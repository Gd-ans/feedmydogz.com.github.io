What we do

FeedMyDogz signifies the testimony of what we aspire to do. We aim to revolutionize the idea of care for Canine, and to do so, we render food, medical assistance, shelter, adoption, and foster homes for stray dogs. The most important characteristics of our services are to offer food rich in nutrition and keep the nutritional values of the food intact; experienced vets examine the food before it is given to serve. Furthermore, to make the food more effective, we have classified it according to the individual dogs as every dog requires special nourishment according to its age and condition.
In addition to an above-given process, our campaign has devised a two-prong system to help the stray dogs, which are as follows:

The services we render to these innocent creatures are as follows:

  • 1. Nutrition: FeedMyDogz gives emphasis on food rich in nutritional value that helps dogs of all age thrive.
  • 2. Medical assistance: Apart from giving them food, we take care of medical condition of stray dogs by conducting regular medical check-ups and providing them medicine if needs be.
  • 3. Water: Since we don't want to leave any stone unturned in this initiative by assuring we install earthen water bowls at as many locations possible.
  • 4. Shelter: In order to look after stray dogs, we plan to set up shelter homes as well wherein they are well taken care of under the supervision of exerts.

Objective: Why do we support stray?

It started during Covid 19 lockdown when the entire world was in a strict lockdown, and work from home was a new concept for most of us. There was no way we could get out or meet our friends. the only source of connecting with them was on zoom and face time. We passed our time cooking delicious dishes. Stepping out for groceries was the only outing we could do, and one such evening, while we stepped out of a grocery store, we found one stray dog looking for some food; we stopped by and gave him some biscuits and milk, he thanked us with a wagging tail, and we went back home. Two days later, we found him there again; this time, he looked at us and slowly came to us, limping his way. We noticed he was bleeding, and we immediately got some medications, cleaned his wound, gave him first aid, got him some eatables and milk, and put them in a corner outside of the shopping complex. That night we were apprehensive for him and went there the following day and took him to a veterinary doctor. A day prior, he got injured while looking around for food and fell in a pit; the onlookers confirmed that we knew he needed more love and attention. Our New friend Simba, as we named him, became our project for a few days to take care of; we assured that we got him treated and then got some earthen pots for his food near the place he used to stay, after a few days we found some more of his friends around who were equally hungry and helpless. Slowly, our evening routines comprised going to feed them, giving us immense pleasure and satisfaction. Gradually our group of dog babies was increasing, and it was becoming burdensome for us to keep up with the cooking of their meals. We shared in a group of our friends about the location, and they started helping with the food supplies to cover as many as 50 dogs in our neighborhoods.

We came across many people through our social networks who wanted to help and be a part of this initiative, like many dog parents. We came through their sick times and asked the VET what food could help them recover fast; some needed medical aid from time to time, their hygiene was another issue we faced, we timely ensured we applied anti tick powders or sprays on them.
After doing our research and constant exchange of love, we already have over 150 dogs we feed every day. We want to make sure we can provide enough support and proper nutrition for the furry babies.

How Covid-19 affected stray dogs across the world?

COVID-19 could be very serious for some people. In some cases, hospitalization can mean that the dog must be taken to a shelter. The challenges of caring for and feeding pets in countries with no animal welfare facilities are very challenging. An increasing number of animals are being left at shelters due to either the incorrect belief that animals can catch COVID-19 or the fact that their owners are unable to take care of them. These are challenging times where it will consider urgent action. FeedMyDogz helps stray dogs in these difficult times by feeding them whenever possible, fighting for the end of the dog, and assisting local shelters to deal with the difficult situations they face.

Stray dogs are suffering from hunger and thirst. Even though all countries are slowly lifting their lockdown regulations, we are still far from living everyday lives. Even though the COVID-19 crises have affected animals, they can't voice their pain. Stray dogs do not belong to anyone but everyone. Our responsibility is to ensure their survival in this situation.

FeedMyDogz is working to ensure that no stray animals were left behind since the COVID-19 pandemic spread and the subsequent nationwide lockdown. Strays find it challenging to find food, with restaurants closing their shutters and food trucks parking at homes. They don't have anything to eat and not even the leftovers food they are supposed to rely on.

These dogs have relied on the kindness of a large group of people. They can be pretty stressed if they don't have anyone to care for them for so long. Humans have played an essential role in their well-being, as well as feeding stray dogs.

We have outlined a few points that we can use to assist strays during the lockdown.

  • It can be a satisfying meal for hungry stray dogs if you make a few chapattis or some rice with some milk.
  • Place a small water bowl outside your home. Animals will all be able to quench themselves as the summer progresses
  • Fostering animals at local shelters can help reduce the current resource crunch these organizations are facing.
  • It makes a huge difference to give away masks, hand sanitizers, and pet food, as well as other necessities.
  • Donating some caring in terms of any required things to some animal welfare NGOs would be appreciable.
